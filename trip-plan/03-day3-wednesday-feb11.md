- **What you'll see:** Sea turtles (common), octopus, excellent tropical fish diversity

## Old Lahaina Luau â€” What to Expect

**Why this luau:**
- Rated **#1 luau on Maui** consistently for decades
- Most authentic and traditional (not a flashy Polynesian revue)
- Focus on Hawaiian culture specifically
- Intimate oceanfront setting
- All-you-can-eat traditional Hawaiian feast
- Live Hawaiian music throughout

**Cost:**
- ~$230/adult
- ~$160/child (ages 2-12)
- **Total for your family: ~$620**
- Includes everything: feast, show, lei greeting, open bar (mai tais, beer, wine, cocktails for adults), non-alcoholic drinks

**Reservation:**
- **Book 2-3 months in advance** â€” it WILL sell out for February
- Book at [oldlahainaluau.com](https://oldlahainaluau.com)
- Choose "Traditional Seating" (on floor mats with backrests) or "Table Seating" (at regular tables). Table seating is easier for families.
- **Arrive early** â€” Best seats (closest to the stage and ocean) are first-come, first-served within your seating category

**Schedule:**
1. **Arrival (5:15 PM)** â€” Lei greeting, welcome mai tai (or juice)
2. **Pre-show activities (5:15-6:00 PM):**
   - Watch the **imu ceremony** (unearth the kalua pig from the underground oven)
   - Learn traditional Hawaiian arts and crafts (lei making, hula lessons, spear throwing, temporary tattoos)
   - Explore the cultural village
   - Stroll the oceanfront lawn
   - Bar opens (mai tais, Maui Brewing beer, wine, cocktails, juice, soda)
3. **Feast begins (6:00 PM):**
   - Buffet opens by seating section
   - All-you-can-eat traditional Hawaiian foods (see menu below)
4. **Hula show (7:00 PM):**
   - ~2 hours of traditional Hawaiian hula and storytelling
   - Journey through Hawaiian history from ancient times to modern day
   - Multiple hula styles (ancient kahiko and modern auana)
   - Live Hawaiian band
   - Male and female dancers
   - Dramatic fire knife finale (though less emphasized than at other luaus)

**The Feast (All-You-Can-Eat Buffet):**

## Practical Notes

- **Snorkeling timing:** Morning (8:00-11:00 AM) has the best water conditions â€” calm winds, flat water, best visibility
- **Ulua Beach parking:** Small lot fills up by 9:30-10:00 AM. Arrive by 8:45 AM if doing Option B
- **Sunscreen reapplication:** You'll be in the water for 2 hours. Reapply reef-safe sunscreen every 90 minutes
- **Driving tip:** The drive from Ka'anapali to South Maui goes through the Ma'alaea area on Highway 30. It's scenic (ocean views, sometimes whales visible from the road!) but can be slow in afternoon traffic heading back. Allow extra time for the return drive.
- **McGregor Point:** On the drive between Ma'alaea and Ka'anapali, there's a lookout on the left side of the road (heading north back). One of the best whale-watching spots on Maui. Worth a 5-min stop on the way back if you have time before the luau.
- **Hydration:** South Maui is the driest part of Maui. Bring plenty of water from the condo.
- **Lunch portions:** Don't eat too much at lunch â€” save your appetite for the massive luau feast!
- **Luau arrival:** Arrive at 5:15 PM (when gates open) to get the best seats within your seating category and enjoy all pre-show activities

## World Whale Day Community Day Festival

**What it is:**
A FREE outdoor festival celebrating humpback whales and marine conservation, hosted by Pacific Whale Foundation. This is the 45th annual event and falls perfectly during your trip.

**Location:** Ma'alaea Harbor Shops & Pacific Whale Foundation headquarters (300 Ma'alaea Rd, Wailuku)

**Events & Activities (typically 10 AM - 4 PM):**
- **Marine science stations** — Touch whale bones, learn about whale anatomy, meet marine biologists
- **Keiki (kids) activity zone** — Crafts, face painting, whale-themed games. Perfect for a 9-year-old.
- **Hawaiian cultural demonstrations** — Hula, lei making, traditional navigation
- **Food vendors** — Local food trucks and stands
- **Live music** — Hawaiian bands
- **Educational talks** — Short presentations about whale conservation
- **Photo ops** — Life-size whale cutouts, marine-themed backdrops

**Cost:** FREE (donations to Pacific Whale Foundation accepted)

**Why go:** It's hands-on, educational, and fun for kids. Your daughter can learn about the whales you've been seeing all week. Plus it's free and casual — you can leave whenever you want.

## Maui Ocean Center

Right next door to the World Whale Day event. Hawaii's premier aquarium.

**Hours:** 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM daily
**Cost:** ~$40/adult, ~$30/child (ages 3-12)
**Time needed:** 1.5-2 hours

**Highlights:**
- **Living Reef exhibit** — Walk-through tunnel surrounded by sharks, rays, and tropical fish
- **Humpback whale exhibit** — 3D humpback whale display, whale sounds, interactive learning
- **Touch pools** — Sea stars, sea cucumbers, urchins (kids love this)
- **Turtle Lagoon** — Green sea turtles swimming overhead
- **Open Ocean exhibit** — Tiger sharks, hammerheads, manta rays, giant trevally
- **Jellyfish exhibit** — Mesmerizing moon jellies

**For a 9-year-old:** This is a hit. The tunnel is the highlight, and the touch pools are interactive.

**Tickets:** Buy online in advance for a small discount and guaranteed entry time.

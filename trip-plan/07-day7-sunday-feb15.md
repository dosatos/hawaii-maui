**Whale Watching from Ka'anapali ‚Äî What to Expect:**

**February = PEAK whale season!** Humpback whales migrate to Maui's warm waters to mate and give birth.

**What you'll see:**
- üêã **Humpback whales** - Almost guaranteed sightings in February
- **Breaching** - Whales launching out of the water
- **Tail slaps** - Powerful tail hitting the water surface
- **Pectoral fin slaps** - Flippers slapping the water
- **Spy hopping** - Whales poking their heads vertically out of water to look around
- **Mother-calf pairs** - Common in February (calves born Dec-Feb)
- **Blows (spouts)** - Water vapor exhaled from blowholes

**Tour details:**
- **Departure:** Ka'anapali Beach area (super convenient from your hotel!)
- **Duration:** 2 hours (1:00-3:00 PM)
- **Type:** Likely catamaran or similar tour boat
- **Cost:** ~$135-180 for family of 3 (already booked!)
- **Narration:** Naturalist guide explaining whale behavior
- **Hydrophone:** Many tours have underwater microphones to hear whale songs

**What to bring:**
- üì∑ **Camera/phone** - Whales can be close!
- üï∂Ô∏è **Sunglasses & hat**
- üß¥ **Sunscreen** - Reapply on the boat
- üíß **Water bottle**
- üß• **Light jacket** - Can be breezy/cool on the water, especially if rainy
- üíä **Motion sickness medication** - Take 30-60 min BEFORE departure if prone to seasickness
- üç¨ **Light snack** - Ginger candies help with nausea

**Important tips:**
- **Rain doesn't matter!** Whales are often MORE active in rain/overcast conditions
- **Take motion sickness meds EARLY** - Dramamine, ginger, wristbands
- **Stay on the shady side** of the boat if it's sunny
- **Listen to the naturalist** - They'll spot whales and explain behavior
- **Be patient** - Whales surface when they want to, not on schedule

**Why whale watching in February is amazing:**
- Peak concentration of humpback whales
- Mother-calf pairs everywhere
- Males singing and competing for females
- Most active whale behavior of the season
- Water is warm and clear

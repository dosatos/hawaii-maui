# Reservations Checklist ‚Äî Book ASAP

**Critical:** Many of these sell out or have limited availability in February (peak season). The earlier you book, the better.

## Priority 1: BOOK IMMEDIATELY

These require advance booking and have limited availability.

### 1. Haleakala Sunrise Reservation ‚≠ê MOST TIME-SENSITIVE

**What:** Sunrise viewing at Haleakala summit (Day 6: Saturday, Feb 14)

**When to book:** Reservations open **60 days in advance at 7:00 AM Hawaii Standard Time (HST)**
- For Feb 14, 2026, that's **Monday, December 15, 2025 at 7:00 AM HST**
- Set an alarm! These sell out in **minutes**

**Where:** [recreation.gov/ticket/facility/253731](https://www.recreation.gov/ticket/facility/253731)

**Cost:**
- $1.00 per vehicle for the sunrise reservation
- $30.00 park entrance fee (good for 3 days, pay at the park)
- **Total: $31**

**Time slot:** Book a reservation window starting between 3:00-7:00 AM. Recommended: 5:00-7:00 AM window.

**Steps:**
1. Create a recreation.gov account NOW (don't wait until booking day)
2. Add a credit card to your account
3. Set an alarm for 6:55 AM HST on December 15, 2025
4. Log in at 6:58 AM, refresh the page at exactly 7:00 AM
5. Select your date (Feb 14) and time window, complete purchase immediately
6. Print or save the confirmation email

**Backup plan if it sells out:**
- Try for Feb 13 or Feb 15 instead
- Go for sunset instead (no reservation required, also beautiful)
- Go midday to hike the crater without sunrise crowds

### 2. Old Lahaina Luau ‚≠ê HIGH PRIORITY

**What:** Traditional Hawaiian luau (Day 7: Sunday, Feb 15) ‚Äî SPLURGE #2

**When to book:** 2-3 months in advance (opens ~6 months ahead, sells out weeks in advance for peak dates)
- **Book by mid-November 2025** at the latest
- Sunday nights book up fastest

**Where:** [oldlahainaluau.com](https://www.oldlahainaluau.com/)

**Cost:** ~$230/adult, ~$160/child (ages 2-12)
- **Total for family of 3: ~$620**

**What to choose:**
- **Date:** Sunday, February 15, 2026
- **Seating:** "Table Seating" (easier for families) or "Traditional Seating" (on mats with backrests)
- **Arrival time:** Gates open at 5:15 PM; show starts around 7:00 PM

**Tips:**
- Book the earliest dinner seating if multiple times are offered
- Arrive as early as the gates open for best seats (first-come within your seating category)
- Add parking pass if offered (it's free but sometimes requires a "ticket")

**Notes:**
- Confirmation will be emailed
- Cancellation policy: Check their website (typically 48-72 hours for refund)

---

### 3. Atlantis Submarine ‚≠ê MODERATE PRIORITY

**What:** Real submarine dive to 100-130 feet (Day 4: Thursday, Feb 12) ‚Äî SPLURGE #1

**When to book:** 1-2 weeks in advance
- Can book sooner, but availability is usually decent

**Where:** [atlantisadventures.com/maui](https://www.atlantisadventures.com/maui)

**Cost:** $179/adult, $80/child (12 & under)
- **Total for family of 3: ~$438**

**Requirements:**
- Minimum height: 36 inches (your 9-year-old is fine)
- Must be able to climb a vertical 10-step ladder independently

**What to choose:**
- **Date:** Thursday, February 12, 2026
- **Departure location:** Lahaina Harbor
- **Time:** Morning departures (9-11 AM) have best underwater visibility
- **Operating days:** Confirm they're running on Feb 12 (typically 4-5 days/week)

**Tips:**
- Check their operating schedule first (they may not run every day)
- Confirm Lahaina Harbor operations (submarine resumed there in Dec 2025)
- Book online for small discount vs. booking at the harbor
- Arrive 30 minutes before your departure time

### 4. Wai'anapanapa State Park (Black Sand Beach)

**What:** Entry reservation for black sand beach on Road to Hana (Day 5: Friday, Feb 13)

**When to book:** 2+ weeks in advance
- Opens 30 days ahead, but book as soon as you can

**Where:** [gowaianapanapa.com](https://www.gowaianapanapa.com/) or Hawaii DLNR reservation system

**Cost:**
- ~$10 per vehicle
- ~$5 per person (non-resident)
- **Total: ~$25 for your family**

**What to choose:**
- **Date:** Friday, February 13, 2026
- **Time window:** Late morning (11:00 AM - 1:00 PM works with your Road to Hana schedule)

**Notes:**
- Without a reservation, you cannot enter the park
- Bring printed confirmation or have it on your phone
- Park entrance is at Mile Marker 32 on the Hana Highway

**What to choose:**

- **Time:** Morning (8-10 AM start) for best conditions
- **Location:** Ka'anapali or nearby beach

**Alternative if surf isn't appealing:**
- **Kayak + snorkel tour:** Similar cost, paddle along coast and snorkel with turtles

### 6. Car Rental

**When to book:** ASAP ‚Äî February is peak season

**Where to compare prices:**
- [Costco Travel](https://www.costcotravel.com/) (best rates if you have membership)
- [AutoSlash](https://www.autoslash.com/) (finds discounts and tracks price drops)
- [Kayak](https://www.kayak.com/cars)
- Directly with rental companies (Enterprise, National, Alamo, etc.)

**What you need:**
- **Pickup:** Kahului Airport (OGG), Monday, February 9, 2026, ~2-3 PM (adjust to your arrival time)
- **Return:** Kahului Airport (OGG), Monday, February 16, 2026, ~10-11 AM (2 hours before your flight)
- **Car type:** Midsize sedan or compact SUV
- **Estimated cost:** $600-900 for the week

**Tips:**
- Book early and monitor for price drops (AutoSlash will email you if the price decreases)
- Decline the GPS (use your phone)
- Check if your credit card provides rental car insurance (many do) so you can decline CDW/LDW
- Add your spouse as an additional driver
- Book through the airport location (not off-airport with shuttle ‚Äî airport is more convenient)

**Insurance note:**
- Check your personal auto insurance policy ‚Äî it may cover rental cars
- Check your credit card benefits ‚Äî many cards (like Chase Sapphire, Amex Platinum) provide primary or secondary rental car coverage
- Only purchase the rental company insurance if you're not covered elsewhere
